About Me

Work in progress...

Hello!

I am currently enrolled in Visiting Student Research Program (VSRP) working with Prof. Ravi Joshi at the Indian Institute of Astrophyiscs (IIA), Bengaluru. While at IIA, my work is focused on understanding the effects of Active Galactic Nuclei (AGNs) on the process of star formation in the host galaxy.

I have received my Integrated Bachelor of Science and Master of Science (BS-MS) degree from the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Pune. For my Master's Thesis (MS), I worked under the supervision of Prof. Andrea Ferrara (Scuola Normale Superiore, Pisa) and Prof. Pratika Dayal (University of Groningen) on "Dust Obscuration in the Most Massive Galaxies in the Epoch of Reionization (EoR)." This project was conducted in the light of recent James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) observations. These observations predicted an unexpected abundance of massive, luminous, blue and metal-free galaxies.

Apart from research and academics, I am deeply interested in studying history and global geopolitics which affect our day to day life. Studying history through the architecture of concurrent times interests me. I have actively participated in General Knowledge quiz competitions from school level to college level and have won various competitions. In my free time, I love playing various outdoor sports and activities.
